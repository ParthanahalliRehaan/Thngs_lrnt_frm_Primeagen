### 🧾 SOC 2 in Plain Words
- **SOC** means *Service Organization Control*.  
- **SOC 2** is a set of rules that companies follow to show they keep customer data safe.  
- It’s like a **report card** for how well a company protects information.  

### 🔑 What It Checks
SOC 2 auditors look at five areas (called “Trust Service Criteria”):
1. **Security** – Are systems protected from hackers?  
2. **Availability** – Is the service reliable and up when customers need it?  
3. **Processing Integrity** – Does the system work correctly without errors?  
4. **Confidentiality** – Is sensitive data kept private?  
5. **Privacy** – Are personal details handled properly?  

### 🏢 Why Companies Care
- Big clients often **require** SOC 2 before they’ll work with a startup.  
- It builds **trust**: customers know their data won’t be misused.  
- It’s not “made up” — it’s an official auditing standard created by the **American Institute of CPAs (AICPA)**.  

### 🎬 Why Primeagen Joked About It
Primeagen was being playful: compliance frameworks can feel **boring and bureaucratic**, so he exaggerated by saying “SOC 2 compliance is made up.” But in reality, it’s very real and important for SaaS companies.

---

Think of SOC 2 like the **locks and alarm system on a house**:  
- The lock = Security  
- The alarm = Availability (alerts if something goes wrong)  
- The blueprint = Processing integrity (house built correctly)  
- Curtains = Confidentiality (privacy inside)  
- Guest rules = Privacy (how visitors’ info is handled)
