---

## 🧩 Phase 1: The 2k‑Line Bliss
- **Greenfield Development** → You’re hacking features fast, no legacy constraints.  
- **Low Cyclomatic Complexity** → Functions are small, dependencies are minimal.  
- **No Architectural Debt Yet** → You haven’t felt the pain of poor abstractions.  
- **Rapid Iteration Mindset** → “Ship it, we’ll refactor later.”  
- **Directory Structure Chaos** → Files live wherever, but it doesn’t matter—everything still fits in your head.  

At this stage, devs are in the **“velocity over discipline” zone**. You don’t care about design patterns, dependency injection, or separation of concerns because the system is still small enough to brute‑force your way through.

---

## ⚡ Phase 2: The 10k‑Line Regret
- **Exponential Technical Debt** → Poor abstractions now multiply across modules.  
- **Tight Coupling** → You realize half your functions depend on global state or implicit side effects.  
- **High Cognitive Load** → No single dev can hold the entire system in working memory.  
- **Refactor Hell** → Changing one function breaks five unrelated modules due to hidden dependencies.  
- **Version Control Pain** → Merge conflicts spike because the codebase lacks modular boundaries.  
- **Scaling Bottlenecks** → Performance issues emerge (N+1 queries, unindexed DB calls, blocking I/O).  
- **DevOps Overhead** → CI/CD pipelines start failing because tests aren’t isolated or reproducible.  
- **Architecture Regret** → You wish you had chosen microservices, or at least a layered architecture, instead of a monolith with spaghetti imports.  

This is the **“oh no, we should’ve asked those questions earlier” moment.** The regret comes from realizing that the shortcuts taken in the first 2k lines now require **massive rewrites, refactors, or architectural migrations**.

---

## 🧠 Translation in Primeagen’s Tone
- At 2k lines: *“You’re vibing, shipping features, feeling like a 10x dev.”*  
- At 10k lines: *“You’re crying in the shower because every PR is a refactor disguised as a feature.”*  

---

👉 In dev jargon: Primeagen is pointing out the **transition from low‑entropy prototyping to high‑entropy maintenance**, where **architectural debt compounds non‑linearly** and forces developers to confront the consequences of ignoring system design principles.
