- **How it works**: Developers write code to control agents in a chaotic shared arena.

## 🟩 AD Lines in Neovim (Git sense)
- **Full form**: **Added/Deleted lines**.  
- **What they are**: Markers in the gutter showing Git changes.  
- **Colors**: Green (added), Red (deleted), Blue (modified).  
- **Plugin**: Typically `gitsigns.nvim`.  
- **Purpose**: Lets you track changes inline without running `git diff`.  

---

## 📏 AD Lines in Neovim (Primeagen’s usage)
- **Full form (his usage)**: **Align/Discipline lines**.  
- **What they are**: Huge vertical lines spanning the entire page, placed at a fixed column (e.g., 80 or 100).  
- **Implementation**: Configured with Neovim’s `colorcolumn` option (`set colorcolumn=80`) or styled with plugins.  
- **Purpose**:  
  - Acts as a **visual ruler**.  
  - Enforces line length discipline (don’t exceed 80–100 chars).  
  - Provides a strong visual anchor across the buffer.  

---

## 📐 Indent Guides (the other vertical lines)
- **What they are**: Vertical lines marking indentation levels.  
- **Plugins**: `indent-blankline.nvim` or `mini.indentscope`.  
- **Purpose**: Helps visualize nested code blocks and scope depth.  
- **Appearance**: Multiple thin vertical lines aligned with each indent level.
