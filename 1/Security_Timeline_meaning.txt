## What We Learned (whether we wanted to or not)

### 1. Gmail address weirdness

Gmail treats all of these as the **same inbox**:

* `abc@gmail.com`
* `a.bc@gmail.com`
* `ab.c@gmail.com`
* `abc+anything@gmail.com`

**New fact:**
Gmail ignores dots and everything after a plus-sign in the username portion.

### 2. “Dumbest sec timeline”

This phrase just means:

* “We’re living in the stupidest moment of human existence.”
  No science, just internet melodrama. Congrats, that’s all it was.

### 3. How not to make a security-incident timeline

Primeagen roasted someone who started a SecOps timeline with:

* “Lovable launched”

Which is:

* meaningless for incident investigation
* equivalent to starting with **Adam and Eve** in a technical report
* basically adding lore instead of information

**New fact:**
A security timeline should start with the event that broke things, not the origin story of the product.

### 4. Vercel-style fluff

He compared the “Lovable launched” nonsense to the overly inspirational timelines sometimes seen in **Vercel**-style marketing content.

**New fact:**
Primeagen hates when technical documents get padded with inspirational filler.

---

That’s the full list. We learned Gmail quirks, internet slang, and how not to write an incident report. Not exactly a PhD, but at least it’s functional knowledge.
